# Inches from Disaster

All you faithful readers are probably wondering what the "mysterious event" is by now that I referenced in my [CCTV Hacking](./2026-04-10-cctv-hacking.md) story. Why could I possibly want security camera footage? (Other than the fact, that's kind of cool.) Well, let's just say, please drive safely, people.

Let's set the scene. It was a dark and story nigh- wait. It was a sunny, clear morning with excellent visibility. A shock for San Francisco, where the norm is dense fog. It was around 7:50AM on Saturday, on March 28th. My girlfriend and I were walking over to the starting line for the Zoom SF Half Marathon. (Yes, that's yet another story!) At the time we were waiting at the crossroads of Richardson and Lyon where it was currently green for the 3 lanes of vehicles crossing Lyon St. and red for pedestrians crossing Richardson Ave.

After a few seconds, a Muni line pulls up on Richardson Ave. to pick up and drop off passengers. It's slow, as usual, and misses the green light for cars. No big deal, it pulls up to the crosswalk and waits for the next one.

At this time, the vehicle light has been solid red for a few seconds and the pedestrian crosswalk light has turned green. So we start crossing. And keep in mind, we're heading to a half marathon so we considered jogging across as a warm-up, but we can't really see around the bus, so we're a bit more careful. And I have not been able to stop thinking about that moment since.

Literally, right after passing the bus on lane 1, an enormous black SUV completely blows through the red light on lane 2. This is maybe 3 feet away from our faces. I can feel the wind from the truck as it blew by. And when I say it blew, I really mean it. The speed limit for vehicular traffic is 25 mph, and while I am not a police-grade speed camera, I can absolutely tell you with 100% certainty that they were going much faster.

> Afterthought: *I* may not be a police-grade speed camera, but by looking at the footage, I was able to determine a rough speed estimate. Using Google Maps, I found out that the distance to cross the intersection for vehicles is about 80 ft. I stepped through my security video frame-by-frame and determined the start and end timestamps, which made the time they crossed the intersection about 1.6 seconds. After doing some conversions, that comes out to over 34 miles per hour.
